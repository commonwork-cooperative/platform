# Contributing to CommonWork

I am a chemical engineer, not a software engineer. I built this because I understand systems and I have been thinking about this particular system for a long time. The technical layer is where I need help.

Here is where things stand and what I am actually looking for.

## What exists right now

The cooperative is real. Colorado Article 56 Cooperative Corporation, Entity ID 20261300628, federal EIN, adopted bylaws, cooperative bank account, Stripe payment integration, verified .coop domain. The governance architecture is more detailed than most production software projects. The legal foundation is solid.

What does not exist yet: a single line of production code.

That is intentional. I would rather have the right people involved in the architecture decisions before anyone writes the wrong code than have to undo something built in the wrong direction.

## What I am building

Eight pillars, but one comes first: **CW Freelance**. A cooperative freelance platform where workers keep 92% of every transaction. No geography dependency. No physical infrastructure. The simplest vertical to prove the economic model before anything else launches.

Everything else comes after the first one works: CW Deliver, CW Rides, CommonTable, AnonConnect, TruthLayer, CW Spaces, CommonWork Open University.

## What decisions are still open

Most of them. That is the point of posting this now.

The stack for CW Freelance MVP is undecided. The smart contract architecture for CommonCoin on Algorand is undecided. The data model for the Worker Assembly voting system is undecided. The API design is undecided.

What is decided: Algorand for CommonCoin (proof-of-stake, $0.001 fees, 4-second settlement, US-regulated), cooperative governance on-chain for credential verification, and the economic model (92% to workers, 8% split across operations, legal defense fund, mutual aid, TruthLayer, and Open University).

## What I am looking for

Engineers who want to shape the architecture, not inherit it. That window is open right now and it will not stay open long.

Specifically useful backgrounds: full-stack web, Algorand smart contracts, distributed systems, payment processing, platform security, and anyone who has built something at scale and learned what not to do.

Less useful right now: front-end polish, mobile apps, AI/ML. Those matter later. The foundation comes first.

## How to get involved

Open an issue with what you are thinking. Ask a hard question. Tell me what you see that I am missing. I have been thinking about this for a long time and I have blind spots I do not know about yet.

Or email admin@commonwork.coop directly. If you have a specific background and a genuine question about whether there is a fit, I would rather have that conversation than not.

## What contributors get

Founding worker-member equity. The same democratic rights as every other member: one vote in the Worker Assembly, patronage dividends when the platform generates revenue, and a permanent stake in what gets built. The governance documents are on the website. Read them. They are serious.

This is not volunteer work with vague promises attached. It is cooperative ownership with legal structure behind it.

## One thing to know going in

I will tell you what I do not know. I expect the same. The fastest way to build something bad is for everyone involved to pretend they have answers they do not have.
